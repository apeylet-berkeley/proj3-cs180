A.1 — Shoot the Pictures

Setup. I fix the center of projection and rotate the camera to capture overlapping views (≈40–70% overlap). I avoid strong lens distortion, shoot quickly to keep exposure consistent, and prefer static scenes with texture.
  • Rotate in place (no translation) → projective relationship holds.
  • Overlap ~40–70% ensures enough shared features.
  • Lock exposure/focus when possible to minimize drift.
  • Avoid fisheye; moderate/wide lenses are fine.

B.1 — Harris Corner Detection & ANMS Deliverable

What I did. I implemented single-scale Harris (Sobel gradients, structure tensor smoothing) and selected peaks via NMS, then applied Adaptive Non-Maximal Suppression (ANMS) to keep well-distributed strong corners. I visualize corners before and after ANMS.

What was tricky. Picking thresholds that generalize: low threshold → cluttered; high threshold → too few corners. ANMS alleviates this by spreading features even in textured regions.

B.2 — Feature Descriptor Extraction Deliverable

What I did. For each keypoint, I extract a 40×40 grayscale patch, apply a light Gaussian blur, then downsample to an 8×8 descriptor and bias/gain-normalize (mean 0, std 1). Rotation invariance is not used as required.

What was tricky. Sampling too small (e.g., straight to 8×8) made descriptors brittle. The 40×40 → blur → 8×8 pipeline produced much more stable matches.

B.3 — Feature Matching (Lowe Ratio) Deliverable

What I did. I compute SSD distances between descriptors and apply Lowe’s ratio test (nearest/second-nearest). I visualize all accepted matches and, separately, the RANSAC inliers.

What was tricky. The ratio threshold is sensitive: I used 0.70–0.75; lower yields cleaner matches but fewer inliers; higher finds more pairs but risks outliers.

B.4 — 4-Point RANSAC & Automatic Mosaics Deliverable

What I did. I estimate robust homographies with 4-point RANSAC (DLT inside), then warp/blend to make mosaics. I compare manual (Part A) vs automatic (Part B) stitching over three pairs.

What was tricky. Huge images caused RAM spikes during warping/blending. I fixed it with safe downscaling, float32 tensors, and fast feathered alphas.
